Lamp

Agency: Crispin Porter + Bogusky
Director: Spike Jonze
Campaign: Unböring
Year: 2002

This hilarious campaign won a cannes lions international ad festival film price

The spot opens with a red lamp, obviously from Ikea, in the corner of a room. Sad piano music pulls on the heartstrings as the lamp gets cast out on to the street. It’s slow, deliberate and agonizing, before flipping a switch. A peculiar Swedish man approaches the camera and says: “Many of you feel bad for this lamp. That is because you crazy . It has no feelings, and the new one is much better.”

YEah IKEA tricks viewers into feeling sorry for furniture!!

I was mind blown watching this TV commercial, These story-driven commercials don’t just sell a product; they sell an experience, an emotion. Brands like IKEA master the art of storytelling in their ads often find themselves etched in the collective memory of their audience, reaping benefits long after the commercial has aired.

And as a video marketer, I have always been interested in employing the art of storytelling when it comes to creating content and video ads for brands, for me it's a strategic choice that boasts tangible benefits.

ADRAR N BAYA

Director: Azzedine Meddour
Year: 1997
Time: 1h56min

The film explore the theme of Berber permanence and resistance, using the story of Baya, a character who exemplifies the ability to resist while staying true to traditions, language, and thought. Azzedine Meddour addresses contemporary Algeria through Baya's intransigence, portraying it as an example for the entire people. The narrative, set almost a century ago, highlights Baya's determination and the peasants' collective effort to transform an arid mountain into a village, symbolizing resistance, courage, and perseverance.

Baya's struggle, as the daughter of a spiritual leader, becomes a fight for the survival of essential values, even at the cost of imposing sacrifices on her family. The film is described as a hymn to resistance, where ancestral references serve as a means of exorcising the forces of evil affecting humanity. Despite challenges during filming, including financial constraints and a tragic explosion, the director perseveres with the support of the team, merging reality and fiction.

This artwork film, much like the novel " la colline oubliée", stands out not only for its cinematic qualities but for its necessity.

"Adrar n baya" has a real personal value to me because my childhood was accompanied by my aunt’s stories about it's various behind-the-scenes and unfortunate incidents , in addition to the participation of one of our family members in this historical film. That old man with long white hair who I thought was Dumbledore from "Harry potter".
